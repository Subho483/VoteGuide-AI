The Election Process

6 Simple steps to understand how democracy works.

📝

1. Voter Registration

Register online or offline via election commission. Check validity of voter ID.

🤝

2. Candidate Nomination

Candidates file their nomination papers, declare assets, and undergo scrutiny.

📢

3. Campaign Period

Parties hold rallies and share manifestos. A silent period applies 48 hrs before voting.

🗳️

4. Voting Day

Go to the polling booth, show ID, press the button on the EVM (or ballot), and get the ink mark.

🔢

5. Counting

EVMs are opened securely and votes are counted in the presence of election observers.

🏆

6. Results

Candidates with majority votes are declared winners and form the government.

🚨 Fake News Awareness

Misinformation damages democracy. Learn how to identify and stop it.

🏛️

Official Sources Only

Always verify election news and dates directly with the official Election Commission portal. Avoid forwarding WhatsApp rumors.

🕵️

Cross-Check Fact-checkers

If a claim seems outrageous, check reputable fact-checking platforms. Be cautious of emotionally manipulative headlines.

📸

Beware Fake Media

Photos and audio can be artificially generated (Deepfakes). Pay attention to irregularities and seek original video contexts.

Frequently Asked Questions

You can register online via your national election portal or physically by submitting the required forms at a local electoral office.

You typically need a Voter ID card, Passport, Driving License, or other government-approved photo ID.

Usually, you can only vote in the constituency you are registered in. However, postal ballots or special transfers might be available for specific categories.

In most democracies, voting is a civic right, not a legal compulsion. However, participating strengthens democracy.
